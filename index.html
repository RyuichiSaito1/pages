Ryuichi Saito

I’m currently a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Tsukuba, Japan, advised by Sho Tsugawa. Starting in February 2025, l am also visiting KU Leuven in Belgium. Before studying abroad, I worked in private equity investment and software engineering at SoftBank Corp. in Tokyo until January 2025. My research focuses on applying AI to the social sciences. I have published three papers. All the articles focus on citizens’ sentiments during the Coronavirus pandemic using Transformer-based large language models from a psychological point of view. Outside of work, I enjoy horse racing, exploring city life, and reading history books.

Research

My research seeks to understand human behavior and social phenomena using AI. Specifically include:

  • Natural Language Processing
  • Computational Social Science
  • AI and Society
